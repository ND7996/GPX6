\section{EVB Formalism and Free-Energy Reconstruction}

In the standard two-state empirical valence bond formalism,\cite{warshel_empirical_1980} the reacting system is described by
\begin{equation}
\mathbf{H}_{\mathrm{EVB}}=
\begin{pmatrix}
\epsilon_1 & H_{12} \\
H_{12} & \epsilon_2 + \alpha
\end{pmatrix},
\label{eq:evb_hamiltonian}
\end{equation}
where $\epsilon_1$ and $\epsilon_2$ are the reactant and product diabatic energies, $H_{12}$ is the off-diagonal coupling, and $\alpha$ is the empirical state-energy shift. The ground-state energy is
\begin{equation}
E_g=\frac{\epsilon_1+\epsilon_2+\alpha}{2}
-\sqrt{\left(\frac{\epsilon_2-\epsilon_1+\alpha}{2}\right)^2+H_{12}^2}.
\label{eq:evb_ground_state}
\end{equation}

Free-energy perturbation (FEP) sampling was performed on mapping potentials rather than directly on the mixed EVB surface.\cite{marelius_q_1999,oanca_efficient_2023} For two states,
\begin{equation}
V_m=\sum_i\lambda_i^m\epsilon_i,\qquad \sum_i\lambda_i^m=1,
\label{eq:evb_mapping}
\end{equation}
with $\boldsymbol{\lambda}=(1,0)$ and $(0,1)$ denoting the pure reactant and product mapping potentials, respectively. The energy-gap coordinate is $X=\epsilon_1-\epsilon_2$. The free-energy increment between adjacent windows is
\begin{equation}
\Delta G_{m\rightarrow m+1}=-\beta^{-1}\ln\left\langle
\exp[-\beta(V_{m+1}-V_m)]\right\rangle_m,
\label{eq:fep_increment}
\end{equation}
and the accumulated value is $\Delta G_m=\sum_{n=0}^{m-1}\Delta G_{n\rightarrow n+1}$. The end-to-end value for the 51-window mapping is denoted $\Delta G_{\lambda}$. It is a mapping diagnostic between the unmixed diabatic end states, not a second estimate of the EVB reaction free energy $\Delta G^{\circ}$.

The EVB free-energy profile was reconstructed by reweighting sampled configurations to $E_g$ along $X$,
\begin{equation}
G(X)=\Delta G_m-\beta^{-1}\ln\left\langle
\delta(X'-X)\exp[-\beta(E_g(X')-V_m(X'))]\right\rangle_m.
\label{eq:evb_reweighting}
\end{equation}
$\Delta G^{\circ}$ is the difference between the product and reactant minima of $G(X)$, whereas $\Delta G^{\ddagger}$ is the difference between the profile maximum and the reactant minimum. Q free-energy perturbation (QFEP) and QMapper implement this reconstruction by binning the energy-gap coordinate, combining overlapping mapping-window contributions, and applying the calibrated Hamiltonian. In the present calculations, Qdyn sampled the mapping potentials with the FEP-file off-diagonal term set to zero; EVB mixing and the calibrated state-energy shift were applied during QMapper analysis.\cite{marelius_q_1999,PurgBauer2017Qtools,oanca_efficient_2023}
