\caption[Stability effects of cumulative mutations during directed evolution of mouse and human GPX6]{Stability effects of cumulative mutations during directed evolution of mouse and human GPX6. Heatmaps display $\Delta\Delta G^{*}$ (kcal mol$^{-1}$) for (a) mouse and (b) human GPX6 variants across mutation levels. Positive values (red) indicate destabilizing mutations, while negative values (blue) indicate stabilizing mutations relative to the wild-type enzyme. \revadd{This sign convention is analogous to the main-text energetic maps in that positive values denote an unfavorable increase in free energy; here, however, $\Delta\Delta G^{*}$ describes folded-state stability rather than activation or reaction free energy.} Rows represent individual mutations, sorted by increasing distance from the active site (Cys/Sec49). Columns show cumulative mutation levels (level~0 = single mutant; higher levels include all preceding mutations). The coloured badges on the right indicate the number of local hydrogen bonds formed by each mutation when introduced alone. Grey cells denote combinations not observed at that level.}
\label{fig:heatmap_levels}
\end{figure}

    \caption[ProteinMPNN triangular sequence-space projection]{Geometric projection of ProteinMPNN-generated sequence ensembles in triangular sequence space. 
    The plot positions ProteinMPNN-designed sequences according to their normalized Levenshtein distances from three reference points: the mouse GPX6 wild-type structure (PDB 7FC2, bottom-left, blue vertex), the AlphaFold-predicted human GPX6 wild-type (bottom-right, orange/red vertex), and the node~25 ancestral sequence from the mammalian GPX6 reconstruction of Rees et al. (green, apex). The three distances were converted into barycentric weights with the same softmax projection used in the main text ($\tau=1.0$). Blue points represent sequences generated from mouse-derived backbones, while orange/red points correspond to sequences generated from human-derived backbones. The dense central cloud of intermixed points highlights substantial overlap between the two directional designs, with only limited extension toward the ancestral node. \revadd{Because ProteinMPNN was used here as a fixed-backbone design probe rather than as a phylogenetic reconstruction model, proximity to node~25 should be interpreted as structural compatibility with ancestral-like sequence features rather than as recovery of the ancestral sequence.} This projection reveals the extent of ancestral sequence recovery achieved by ProteinMPNN when using EVB-derived intermediate and endpoint backbone structures along the mouse-to-human and human-to-mouse trajectories.}
