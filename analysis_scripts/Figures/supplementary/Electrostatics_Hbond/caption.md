\caption[Mutation-level $\Delta\Delta G^*$ profiles]{
\textbf{$\Delta\Delta G^*$ profiles across all mutation levels for Human and Mouse GPX6 variants.}
Heatmaps showing the computed change in folding free energy ($\Delta\Delta G^*$, kcal~mol$^{-1}$) relative to the catalytic-residue-swapped background for all amino acid variants in Human (top) and Mouse (bottom) GPX6, across mutation levels 0--18 and 0--20, respectively.
A positive $\Delta\Delta G^*$ indicates destabilisation relative to that background, while a negative $\Delta\Delta G^*$ indicates stabilisation.
Rows are sorted by hydrogen bond count and level-0 $\Delta\Delta G^*$, with H-bond counts shown as coloured badges (right; purple~=~0 to orange~=~5). Here, level~0 denotes the first noncatalytic mutation after the catalytic-residue replacement at position~49, and the corresponding $\Delta\Delta G^*$ values are annotated numerically in the first column.
Red and blue cells indicate destabilising and stabilising mutations, respectively; grey cells indicate mutations absent at that level.
Active-site H-bond mutants are marked with $\square$ (plotted left of heatmap).
\textbf{Note: H-bond counts are a geometric descriptor of local interaction density, not a mechanistic claim.}
}
